FAQ

What is a Dat archive?

A Dat archive is a collection of files that can be efficiently shared with others on a peer-to-peer network.

How do I create a Dat archive?

You can use the Beaker browser, the official Dat command line tool, or the official Dat desktop app.

Who can see the archives that I upload to Hashbase?

Free plan

If you use Hashbase's free plan, the Dat URL for all of your archives will be listed on your public profile.

Because of the nature of the Dat peer-to-peer protocol, anyone with the URL for an archive will be able to view and download the archive's files. Upgrade to pro

Hashbase Pro

Archives uploaded with Hashbase Pro are unlisted by default. That means they won't be listed on your public Hashbase profile, and only people who know the Dat URL for your archive will be able to see its files. Upgrade to pro

Can I self host Hashbase?

Yes. The software that powers Hashbase, Hypercloud, is open source and easy to deploy.
